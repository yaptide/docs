This page walks you through defining a complete simulation geometry.
We'll build a setup with three components — a water phantom, a lead collimator with a hole, and a world volume filled with air.

![Geometry Page](/docs/assets/geometry/geometry.png)

## How geometry works

Geometry definition varies between simulation engines:

- **CSG (Constructive Solid Geometry)** — used by SHIELD-HIT12A and FLUKA. Shapes are built from primitive figures (boxes, cylinders, spheres) combined with boolean operators. The order of operations is defined within a **Zone**.
- **Nested volumes** — used by Geant4. Figures are arranged in a tree-like hierarchy, where child volumes sit inside parent volumes.

## Adding figures

We'll create three groups of figures:

- **World box** — air-filled, large enough to contain everything
- **Water phantom** — 8×8×8 cm box
- **Collimator** — two cylinders (outer envelope + inner hole)

### World filled with air

Add a box by selecting **Object > Box** in the menu bar, or clicking **+ BOX** in the FIGURES section of the GEOMETRY tab.

![Add Figure from Object Menu](/docs/assets/geometry/menu_object_add.png)

![Add Figure from FIGURES section](/docs/assets/geometry/figures_add.png)

Create a box spanning from −2 to 10 cm in Z and −5 to 5 cm in X and Y.
Set the **Position** (center) to `(4, 0, 0)` and **dimensions** to `12 × 10 × 10` cm.
Basic dimension units in YAPTIDE are centimetres.
Give the figure a meaningful name like `WorldBox`.

![Object details](/docs/assets/geometry/object_details.png)

The figure appears as cross-sections in the YX, XZ, and ZY planes, plus a 3D projection.

### Water phantom

We'll duplicate the world box and shrink it. Right-click on `WorldBox` in the FIGURES section and click **Duplicate**.

![Duplicate WorldBox](/docs/assets/geometry/duplicate.png)

The copy inherits the name with a `_1` suffix, plus all properties.

![Duplicated WorldBox](/docs/assets/geometry/duplicated.png)

Change the dimensions to `8 × 8 × 8` cm (position stays the same) and rename to `PhantomBox`.

![PhantomBox](/docs/assets/geometry/phantom_box.png)

### Collimator

Add two cylinders — the outer envelope (radius 4 cm, thickness 1 cm) and the inner hole (radius 2 cm).
Both are centred at `(0, 0, 0)`.
Use click-and-drag on the 3D view to rotate and verify they look right.

## Zones (CSG only)

:::note
Zones apply only to SHIELD-HIT12A and FLUKA. In Geant4, material assignment is done directly on each figure. Skip this section if using Geant4.
:::

Zones describe a volume of space by combining figures with boolean operations and assigning a physical material.

:::caution
Each point in space must belong to exactly one Zone. If a volume encloses another, you need to subtract the inner volume from the outer one to avoid overlap.
:::

You can add zones from **Object > Boolean Zone** in the menu, or from the ZONES section of the GEOMETRY tab.

![Add Boolean Zone from Object Menu](/docs/assets/geometry/menu_boolean_zone_add.png)
![Add Boolean Zone from ZONES section](/docs/assets/geometry/boolean_zone_add.png)

:::note
A special "World Zone" representing the entire simulation space is defined by default and cannot be removed.
:::

### Air zones

We need two air zones because the collimator sits inside the world separately from the phantom:

- **MainAirZone** — the world box, minus the phantom box and the outer collimator cylinder, plus the inner cylinder back
- **CollimatorAirZone** — adds back the inner cylinder air

Defining the MainAirZone with boolean operations:

![Defining zone operations for MainZone](/docs/assets/geometry/zone_operations.gif)

CollimatorAirZone:

Add a new zone and assign `PhantomBox` as the figure.
The default material is "Liquid Water" so no change needed.
Change the colour and inspect in the 3D view.
